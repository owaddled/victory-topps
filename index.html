About Victory

In April 1993, Topps Comics launched a line of licensed comics based on concepts from Jack Kirby that fans nicknamed the 'Kirbyverse.' It was the last major project of the King of Comics' life. The majority of the comics were drawn by Steve Ditko, Kirby's counterpart in creating the Marvel Universe. The first issues sold well, but sales declined quickly.

In June 1994, months after Kirby passed away, Topps Comics released Victory #1, a the first issue of a crossover miniseries that aimed to modernize, relaunch and expand the Kirbyverse. Victory had an all-star team: pencils by Keith Giffen, inks by Jimmy Palmiotti, and script by Kurt Busiek, but only one issue was released before the mini-series and all future plans for the Kirbyverse were cancelled.

Thankfully, some original art for later issues of Victory was put up for sale on The Artist’s Choice (archived page via the Wayback Machine). With the help of ChatGPT, I've done a bit of Lanczos resampling on the images from The Artist's Choice and created this web page to host these unpublished pages. .
